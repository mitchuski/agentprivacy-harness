# Field Guide: Preparing a Home for a 72-Hour Power Outage

Prepare a household to pass safely and comfortably through an outage up to 72 hours. Temperate climate, no backup generator. Do sections in order; earlier ones matter most.

## Water
- Store 4 L per person per day = 12 L per person for the full 72 hours.
- Half for drinking; half for cooking and basic hygiene.
- Food-grade containers, out of direct sunlight; replace every 6 months.
- If storm forecast with advance warning, also fill bathtub — bathtub water for flushing toilets and washing, never drinking.

## Food
- Dedicated shelf, non-perishable food for 3 days: canned beans, canned fish, peanut butter, crackers, dried fruit, shelf-stable milk.
- Choose no-cook foods; own a manual can opener (electric useless).
- Eat order: perishable fridge food first, then freezer, then emergency shelf.

## Refrigerator and freezer
- Unopened fridge keeps food safe ~4 hours.
- Full freezer holds safe temperature ~48 hours; half-full only ~24 hours — keep it consolidated and full, even with containers of frozen water.
- Keep both doors closed as much as possible; tape a reminder note to each.
- Discard perishable food over 2 hours above 4°C; when in doubt, throw it out.

## Light
- Primary light: battery-powered LED lanterns or headlamps. Avoid candles entirely — leading cause of house fires during outages.
- At least one light per person, plus two spare battery sets per light.
- One headlamp beside each bed, one lantern in kitchen; place where findable in the dark.

## Heat and safety
- Never run camping stove, barbecue, or generator indoors, in garage, or near window — each emits carbon monoxide, odourless, can be fatal.
- Install at least one battery-powered carbon monoxide alarm on each floor where people sleep.
- In cold, pick one small room on south-facing side, close door, gather everyone; layered clothing and blankets beat any improvised heater.
- If indoor temperature falls below 10°C and you cannot stay warm, relocate to a warming centre or neighbour's home.

## Communication and information
- Keep battery-powered or hand-crank radio; learn local emergency station's frequency before outage.
- Charge every phone and power bank whenever storm forecast; full 20,000 mAh power bank recharges typical phone ~4 times.
- Put phone in low-power mode at start.
- Agree in advance on one out-of-area contact everyone texts — texts beat voice calls on congested networks.

## Medical needs
- First-aid kit and 7-day supply of every prescription; rotate to stay in date.
- Refrigerated medication such as insulin generally lasts at room temperature up to 28 days — confirm your specific drug with a pharmacist beforehand.
- Write pharmacy, doctor, and poison-control numbers on paper; a dead phone is not a phone book.

## When the power returns
- Wait 5 minutes before switching major appliances on, letting grid stabilise.
- Check freezer: food still holding ice crystals can be safely refrozen.
- Restock everything used within one week, starting with water and batteries.
- Write a one-page note on what was missing or awkward; fix at least one gap before next outage.
