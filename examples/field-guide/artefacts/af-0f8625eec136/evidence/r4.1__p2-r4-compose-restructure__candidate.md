# 72-Hour Power-Outage Home Field Guide

Prepares a household to pass safely and comfortably through an outage up to 72 hours. Temperate climate, no backup generator. Do sections in order; earlier ones matter most.

## Water
- Store 4 L/person/day = 12 L/person for the full 72 hours: half drinking, half cooking and basic hygiene.
- Food-grade containers, out of direct sunlight; replace every 6 months.
- Storm forecast with advance warning: also fill bathtub — for flushing toilets and washing, never drinking.

## Food and cold storage
- Dedicated shelf, 3 days non-perishable: canned beans, canned fish, peanut butter, crackers, dried fruit, shelf-stable milk.
- Choose no-cook foods; own a manual can opener (electric useless).
- Eat order: perishable fridge food, then freezer, then emergency shelf.
- Unopened fridge keeps food safe ~4 hours.
- Full freezer holds safe temperature ~48 hours, half-full only ~24 — keep it consolidated and full, even with containers of frozen water.
- Keep both doors closed as much as possible; tape a reminder note to each.
- Discard perishables over 2 hours above 4°C; when in doubt, throw it out.

## Light
- Primary: battery-powered LED lanterns or headlamps. No candles — leading cause of house fires during outages.
- At least one light per person, plus two spare battery sets per light.
- Headlamp beside each bed, lantern in kitchen; place where findable in the dark.

## Heat and safety
- Never run camping stove, barbecue, or generator indoors, in garage, or near a window — each emits odourless, potentially fatal carbon monoxide.
- Battery-powered carbon monoxide alarm, at least one, on each floor where people sleep.
- Cold: pick one small south-facing room, close door, gather everyone; layered clothing and blankets beat any improvised heater.
- Indoors below 10°C and cannot stay warm: relocate to a warming centre or neighbour's home.

## Communication
- Battery or hand-crank radio; learn the local emergency station's frequency before the outage.
- Storm forecast: charge every phone and power bank — a full 20,000 mAh power bank recharges a typical phone ~4 times.
- Phone into low-power mode at start.
- Pre-agree one out-of-area contact everyone texts; texts beat voice calls on congested networks.

## Medical
- First-aid kit plus 7-day supply of every prescription, rotated to stay in date.
- Refrigerated medication such as insulin generally lasts at room temperature up to 28 days — confirm your specific drug with a pharmacist beforehand.
- Pharmacy, doctor, and poison-control numbers on paper; a dead phone is not a phone book.

## When power returns
- Wait 5 minutes before switching major appliances on; let the grid stabilise.
- Freezer food still holding ice crystals can be safely refrozen.
- Restock everything used within one week, water and batteries first.
- Write a one-page note on what was missing or awkward; fix at least one gap before the next outage.
