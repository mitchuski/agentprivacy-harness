# KILLED LEVERS — the universe-builder

A killed lever is a fence, not a footnote. Filed at the same prominence as a
win (GR-6). No seat re-proposes a K-id without new evidence, cited.

Each entry: what it was · why it died · what evidence killed it · what would
re-open it. **Killed ≠ impossible.**

---

## K-1 · Wing-based seat assignment

**The lever.** Assign harness seats by persona `alignment`: mage-aligned
personas take `propose`, swordsman-aligned take `assay`. It is what
`SPECIALISATION.md` asserted, and it is tidy.

**Why it died.** The only worked reference falsifies it. The tigzkp registry
seats `algebraist` (swordsman-aligned) at `propose` and `cipher`
(swordsman-aligned) at `assay` — both algebra seats wear the same wing — and
the instance is coherent and passes its gate.

**Evidence.** The instance's canon registry, read directly; `universe/ERRATA.md`
E-4; `universe/SEATS.md` §2.

**What it teaches.** The wing is a skill affinity. The separation is carried by
the Gap and the algebra, never by the costume. A harness that trusts the
costume has a wing where it needs a Gap — a *worse* failure than having no
rule, because it feels safe.

**Re-opens if.** A future corpus adds a genuine `harness_seat` field and the
wing is shown to co-vary with it across ≥3 independent instances.

## K-2 · Seating `weaver` at the Gap

**The lever.** `weaver`'s glyph is literally ⿻ and its proverb reads like the
hold-apart seat's job description. Seat it there.

**Why it died.** `weaver` is **mage-aligned** — a proposer of plurality.
Seating it at `hold-apart` puts a proposer in the held-apart seat, which is
the precise failure the seat exists to prevent (T2). The glyph is a coincidence
of notation, not an argument.

**Evidence.** `persona/agentprivacy-weaver` frontmatter (`alignment: mage`);
the Gap's formal definition as maximal betweenness centrality `C_B(v)`, which
is `topologist`'s stated equation term.

**What it teaches.** A matching symbol is not a matching mandate. This is the
cheapest way in the whole corpus to put the wrong agent in the one seat that
must be empty of both.

**Re-opens if.** Never, on these grounds. A *different* ⿻-bearing persona with
`alignment: balanced` could be considered on its term, not its glyph.

## K-3 · Treating 🪢 VRC mana as attestation

**The lever.** Mana accrues from real walks and real focus. Let a validated
round mint mana, and let accumulated mana stand as evidence of trust.

**Why it died.** Canon forbids it, and the specification is explicit: 🪢 is
**regime-1 — non-transferable, non-attesting, local colour.** *No surface may
describe 🪢 as proof, stake-weight, or attestation input.* Its implementation
is an integer in local storage.

**Evidence.** `universe/ERRATA.md` E-3; the mana taxonomy in the City grimoire;
the PVM v6 specification's regime note.

**What it teaches.** The mana is what you play with; the VRC is what you prove
with. They share a glyph and nothing else. A fleet that spends mana as
evidence has silently set a factor to zero (T5) and will not notice until the
product collapses.

**Re-opens if.** The regime climbs — witness co-signing, then elapsed-time
proofs. That is a canon change, and canon changes are the First Person's.

## K-4 · Deriving workshop meaning from the 64-blades reference sheet

**The lever.** The sheet enumerates all 64 blades with dimensions and names —
the obvious source for reading a vertex's meaning.

**Why it died.** The sheet is stale: it prints the **pre-lock** seating
(Aletheia=25, Lethe=38), inverted by the v10.4.0 lock, and it carries V38's
dimension list under V25's number.

**Evidence.** `universe/ERRATA.md` E-7. The blades repo's own `blades/README.md`
carries the reseat erratum and says *the lock governs*.

**What it teaches.** The most dangerous kind of wrong. `V38 ⊕ V25 = 63` holds
either way, **so the anchor law cannot catch a transposition** — a passing
gate sat next to a wrong sheet for weeks. Compute dimensions from the bits
(`d₁ Protection = 32 … d₆ Value = 1`, MSB-first), never read them from prose.

**Re-opens if.** The sheet is corrected against the lock and re-verified
bit-by-bit.
