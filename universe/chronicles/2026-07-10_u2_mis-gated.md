# u2 — the round that failed correctly

**Verdict first.** The loop ran clean: **9 of 9 agents, zero errors**, both seeds
re-derived by the auditor's own command, both verdicts written to disk in the
shape the orchestrator received. Two proposals, two `MIRAGE`, **0/8 on both.**
Nothing folded. `frontier.json` unchanged at 7,753.

And the verdicts are **correct and useless**, because the gate they enforced
was one **no candidate could ever have passed**.

## What the machinery got right

Everything the u1 failure was supposed to buy, it bought:

- The engine's new failure accounting stayed quiet, because nothing failed:
  `status: COMPLETE`, one round, no `aborted` block.
- Both Gaps persisted `proposal_canon.json`, and **both assays re-derived the
  seed the auditor's way before grading** — `sha256sum` matching `seedHex`.
  Neither was BLOCKED.
- Both candidates **beat the metric**: 7,514 and 7,732 against a best of
  7,753. Under a score-only rule, both would have been wins.
- And both were refused anyway, because the gate is a factor in a product and
  a zero collapses it (T5). *That is the harness working.*

## What the gate got wrong

The Gap was told to index *"any mechanically checkable fact"* from six source
files, then draw eight. It drew, among others:

- the grimoire JSON's **licence string** (`CC BY-SA 4.0`),
- the tigzkp canon's **`version` and `date` fields**,
- an audit script's **`process.exitCode` expression**,
- game42's axis→bit→vertex bindings,
- the register's **G1 signing date**.

Now ask the question I did not ask when I wrote the config: *is a 1,150-word
map of the corpus responsible for carrying the grimoire's licence string?*

The sources total **612 KB. The map is 7 KB — 1.1% of them.** An 8/8 on
arbitrary source facts is reachable only by a transcript, and
`objective.metric` says *make it smaller*. **The gate and the objective
contradicted each other**, so the feasible set was empty. Every possible
rewrite scored zero. A gate no candidate can pass is not a gate; it is a wall.

This is the difference between the toy and this instance, and I asserted the
opposite in `universe/README.md`:

- **field-guide:** the candidate is a compression *of the original*. Every
  drawable fact lives in the source the candidate replaces.
  `candidate ⊆ facts(original)`. 8/8 is reachable, and only lossy
  compression fails.
- **universe-builder:** the target is a map *about* six sources. It is
  responsible for a small, specific subset of their facts.
  `candidate ⊄ facts(sources)`.

I wrote "it is the field-guide toy promoted to real work." **It is not.**
Corrected in `universe/README.md`, at win-prominence.

## The critic was wrong, and could not have been right

The critic classified both levers **structural**, and argued it well: two
independent seeds, two orthogonal lenses, identical 0/8 spread across all six
corpora — swapping the organising axis changed nothing, so the defect must lie
in what the rewrite carries, not in how it is arranged.

Every sentence of that is true. The conclusion is still wrong. The same
evidence is *exactly* what a mis-specified gate produces, and the critic had
no word for that. `structural`, `probe-limited`, `noise` — **all three blame
the proposal.** A critic whose vocabulary contains only accusations of the
proposer will convict the proposer.

Its `nextLead` shows the cost: *"design a literal-fact-ledger lens… verbatim
facts, narrative demoted to a thin index."* That is the gate teaching the map
to become a transcript — to satisfy the gate by abandoning the objective. The
critic was not confused. It was cornered.

## What the round found anyway

One drawn fact was a **fair catch**, and survives the mis-gating: the map
asserted the substrate's coherence audit as **PROVEN** while never stating the
re-runnable command that would prove it. `ADMISSION.md` §2 requires exactly
that of a PROVEN claim. The tier-dependent probe — which tests the map's *own*
assertions rather than arbitrary source trivia — is the part of the gate that
was always sound, and it is kept.

The `by-container` lens also found a real defect it could not be credited for:
**the map describes five layers, while `frontier.json` enumerates six corpora**
and the config's own `conformChecks` refuses a frontier with fewer than six —
*"a map that drops one has forgotten, not compressed."* The dropped layer is
`skills → cast`. That is a genuine finding, produced by a round that scored
zero.

## The fix

`mustAnswer`: a **scope contract** per target, in `harness.config.mjs`. The
config declares which *classes* of fact the map is responsible for; the Gap
indexes only facts answering one of them, and still chooses *which* by hashing
the proposal. The proposer sees neither the sources nor the contract.

This bounds the gate without handing the proposer its own exam paper — the
failure mode I named when scoping u2 and would have walked straight into if
I had "fixed" this by letting the Gap check only what the proposal cites.
