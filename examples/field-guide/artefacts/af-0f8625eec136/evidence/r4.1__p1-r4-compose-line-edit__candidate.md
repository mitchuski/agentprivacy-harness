# 72-Hour Power-Outage Field Guide

Goal: household passes up to 72 hours of outage safely and comfortably. Temperate climate, no backup generator assumed. Do sections in order; earlier ones matter most.

## Water
- Store 4 L per person daily: 12 L per person over 72 hours.
- Half for drinking; half for cooking and hygiene.
- Food-grade containers, out of direct sunlight; replace every 6 months.
- Given advance storm warning, also fill the bathtub: for flushing toilets and washing, never drinking.

## Food
- Dedicated shelf, 3 days' non-perishables: canned beans, canned fish, peanut butter, crackers, dried fruit, shelf-stable milk.
- Choose no-cook foods; own a manual can opener (electric useless).
- Eat fridge perishables first, then freezer, then emergency shelf.

## Refrigerator and freezer
- Unopened fridge keeps food safe ~4 hours.
- Full freezer stays safe ~48 hours, half-full only ~24; keep it consolidated and full, even with containers of frozen water.
- Keep both doors closed; tape a reminder note to each.
- Discard perishables over 2 hours above 4°C; when in doubt, throw it out.

## Light
- Primary light: battery-powered LED lanterns or headlamps. Avoid candles: leading cause of house fires during outages.
- At least one light per person, two spare battery sets per light.
- Headlamp beside each bed, lantern in kitchen; place all findable in the dark.

## Heat and safety
- Never run camping stove, barbecue, or generator indoors, in garage, or near window: each emits odourless, potentially fatal carbon monoxide.
- Install at least one battery-powered carbon monoxide alarm on each floor where people sleep.
- In cold, gather everyone in one small south-facing room, door closed; layered clothing and blankets beat any improvised heater.
- Below 10°C indoors and unable to stay warm, relocate to a warming centre or neighbour's home.

## Communication
- Keep battery-powered or hand-crank radio; learn local emergency station's frequency before outage.
- Charge every phone and power bank whenever storm forecast; a full 20,000 mAh bank recharges a typical phone ~4 times.
- Phones to low-power mode at outage start.
- Pre-agree one out-of-area contact everyone texts; texts beat voice calls on congested networks.

## Medical
- First-aid kit and 7-day supply of every prescription; rotate to stay in date.
- Refrigerated medication like insulin generally lasts up to 28 days at room temperature; confirm your drug with a pharmacist beforehand.
- Pharmacy, doctor, and poison-control numbers on paper; a dead phone is not a phone book.

## When power returns
- Wait 5 minutes before switching major appliances on, letting grid stabilise.
- Check freezer: food still holding ice crystals can be safely refrozen.
- Restock everything used within one week, starting with water and batteries.
- One-page note on what was missing or awkward; fix at least one gap before next outage.
